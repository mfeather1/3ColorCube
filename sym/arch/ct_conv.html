Corner Twist Conversion
(Method 3)

The corner twist symmetry array does not take into account the corner position so the values are only correct for 16 of the 48 symmetries. However, the other 32 symmetries are partially correct in that cubies that are properly positioned (can be solved by rotating in place) maintain the same twist value for all 48 symmetries. Twist values for the other cubies (not properly positioned) change when the cube is rotated to an axis different from the one that has the 16 correct symmetries, this is the purpose of the conversion.

To properly convert, the two different types of 3-color corner cubies are distinguished as shown below. The red/yellow/blue appears in a clockwise order on each cubie on the first cube and counter-clockwise on the second cube.

The following shows what happens to cubies that are not in proper positions when the cube is rotated. Prior to rotating it can be seen that none of the cubies are twisted (twist is based on the color of the top or bottom face). After cube rotation (press Play), the cubies on the first cube below are rotated clockwise (based on blue facelet) and the cubies on the second cube are rotated counter-clockwise.

The following shows rotation to the other axis. After cube rotation, the cubies on the first cube below are rotated counter-clockwise (based on yellow facelet) and the cubies on the second cube are rotated clockwise.

The following table summarizes the conversion. For Cube Rotation, U->R rotates so the Up face moves to the Right face and U-F rotates so the Up face moves to the Front face. For Cubie & Position Type, 0 is clockwise red/yellow/blue & 1 is counter-clockwise. For Cubie Rotation, CW rotates clockwise and CCW rotates counter-clockwise.
