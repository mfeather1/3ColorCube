HTA - 6-Color Corners (odd case)

In this example the red-orange pair of faces have been completely solved (as 3-color) and the corners have been solved on the remaining faces (as 3-color). To solve the corners as 6-color the waterwheel sequence would be used (because one and only one pair of faces can be solved with half-twists) but it is the yellow-white pair of faces that are solvable with half-twists (and are already solved) which means the cube needs to be turned so they are on the left and right. After applying the waterwheel sequence one misplaced edge facelet (from the red-orange pair) will need to be fixed. The table below the cube shows how to proceed.



1Make setup for waterwheel sequence
2-10Apply waterwheel sequence
11Turn cube over
12-16Apply Sequence 3C-E2 to fix misplaced orange facelet
17Turn cube

After move 10 the corners are solvable (as 6-color) with half-twists only but there is no need to do so at this point. After move 17 the cube is ready to solve the remaining edges (as 3-color). The corners will be solved as 6-color (with half-twists only) after the edges are solved as 3-color.
