5x5 Supercube
X-Centers

The X-Centers on the 5x5 Supercube are solved the same way as the 4x4 Supercube. The following shows a comparison of the 4x4 and 5x5 cubes for the sequence which is used to get all centers on each face pointing in the same direction.


The final step of solving pairs of faces by twisting all four X-Centers on each face as one is also done with the same sequences as the 4x4 cube (which are the same as the 3x3 cube). However, if at the end of a solve there remains a single face with all four X-Centers half-twisted then the procedure is a bit different.

To half-twist all four X-Centers on a single face, first use the 3x3 Supercube Sequence to half-twist all of the centers on the top face including the +Centers and Fixed Center (moves 1-12 on the cube below). At that point, the X-Centers are fixed but the +Centers and Fixed Center are half-twisted so turn the cube so the twisted centers are on the front face and use the sequence to half-twist just the +Centers (14-29). Finally, turn the cube so the twisted centers are on the top face and use the sequence to half-twist all the centers again but this time twist two layers together on each move so that only the Fixed Center is twisted (30-42).
