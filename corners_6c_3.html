6-color Corner Sequence on a 3-Color Solution

Use of a 6-color corner sequence on a cube that is already completely solved as 3-color will misplace some edge facelets (as 3-color). These are easily fixed by using the appropriate edge sequence as shown in the following example. The first 9 moves are the waterwheel sequence, move 10 turns the cube to make the setup for Sequence 3C-E2 which fixes the yellow facelet on the top face and the red facelet on the front face (moves 11-15). The corners are then solved with half-twists (16-18) and the cube is ready for the final step of solving the 6-color edges.



With the parallel sequence, use a similar procedure to fix four misplaced facelets with sequence 3C-E1.
